Mixed Martial Arts

Mixed martial arts (MMA) is a full-contact combat sport that allows striking and grappling, both standing and on the ground, using techniques from various combat sports and martial arts.

Originating in Brazil in the early 20th century, MMA gained widespread popularity in the United States in the 1990s and has since become a global phenomenon.

The Rules

  • No eye gouging, biting, or striking the groin
  • No small joint manipulation or striking the back of the head or spine
  • No headbutting or striking with the knee to the head of a grounded opponent
  • No grabbing the fence or holding onto clothing
  • Athletes must wear approved gloves and shorts, and cannot wear shoes or any other protective gear

The Weight Classes

Weight Class Weight Limit
Strawweight 115 lbs (52.2 kg)
Flyweight 125 lbs (56.7 kg)

Welterweight 170 lbs (77.1 kg)

Light Heavyweight 205 lbs (93.0 kg)
Heavyweight 265 lbs (120.2 kg)

There are eight weight classes for men. Fighters must weigh in before their fights and cannot exceed the weight limit for their weight class. Fighters who miss weight may be fined or face other penalties.

The Fighters

Some of the most popular MMA fighters of all time include Conor McGregor, Georges St-Pierre, Anderson Silva, Jon Jones, and Khabib Nurmagomedov.

However, there are many other talented fighters across all weight classes, each with their own unique skills and fighting styles.

The Events

Some of the biggest MMA events include the UFC, Bellator MMA, and ONE Championship.

These organizations host events all around the world, featuring the best fighters from their respective promotions. Fans can watch these events live or on TV, and can also follow the latest news and updates on social media.
