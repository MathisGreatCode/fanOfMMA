Mixed martial arts


A full-contact combat sport that allows striking and grappling, both standing and on the ground, using techniques from various combat sports and martial arts. Originating in the early 20th century, MMA gained widespread popularity in the United States in the 1990s and has since become a global phenomenon.

Rules of Mixed Martial Arts

Fights are scheduled for 3 or 5 rounds of 5 minutes.

Fighters can win by KO/TKO, submission or judges decision.

Weight classes have to be respected.
4 oz gloves are used.

Grabbing the fence or holding onto clothing are forbidden.

Eye poking, biting, headbutting, groin attacks and striking the back of the head are forbidden.

Knee to the head and vertical elbow on a grounded opponent are forbidden.



*Rules can be slightly different according to the organization/promotion

Weight classes

The day before the fight, fighters have to weigh-in to insure they are under the weight limit of their category.

  • Heavyweight - 265 lbs (120.2 kg)
  • Light Heavyweight - 205 lbs (93.0 kg)
  • Middleweight - 185 lbs (83.9 kg)
  • Welterweight - 170 lbs (77.1 kg)
  • Lightweight - 155 lbs (70.3 kg)
  • Featherweight - 145 lbs (65.8 kg)
  • Bantamweight - 135 lbs (61.2 kg)
  • Flyweight - 125 lbs (56.7 kg)

Organizations

There are many organizations/promotions of MMA around the world. Here are some of the main ones


Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC)

It is the leading MMA organization, has a global fan base and has shaped the sport significantly.
With renowned fighters like McGregor, Jones, Adesanya and Volkanovski the UFC boasts an exceptional talent pool. It has expanded its reach through strategic partnerships and broadcasting events worldwide, elevating MMA to new heights.
Endeavor Holding Group acquired the organization in 2021, with Dana White still serving as the CEO.
However, the UFC receives criticism regarding fighter pay, with concerns about inadequate compensation for their contributions and the risks they face in the Octagon.

Bellator MMA

is a leading fight organization, offering a platform for fighters to showcase their skills.
With a diverse global roster, fans witness a captivating display of martial arts excellence. Bellator nurtures fighters worldwide, including established stars, rising prospects, and international champions. From Patricio "Pitbull" Freire to AJ McKee, their fighters captivate audiences with skill and charisma.
Bellator embraces innovation, seen in events like the Bellator Grand Prix tournaments, and prioritizes fan engagement through unique access and interactive experiences.

ONE Championship

is a Singapore-based mixed martial arts promotion that has quickly gained popularity throughout Asia and beyond. ONE's focus is not only on martial arts competition, but also on promoting values such as respect, honor, and humility. The organization has a strong commitment to safety, with stringent rules and regulations to protect the fighters. ONE features some of the world's top fighters competing in both mixed martial arts or muay-thaï, such as Demetrious Johnson or Rodtang Jitmuangnon including champions from a variety of disciplines.

Professional Fighters League (PFL)

is an organization that is rising fast and strong with its innovative approach and recent star signings such as Francis Ngannou or Cedric Doumbe. The PFL stands out from other MMA promotions with its tournament-style format. Fighters compete in a league structure, earning points based on their performances in each bout. These points determine their eligibility for the playoffs, creating a thrilling and high-stakes environment where every fight matters.
